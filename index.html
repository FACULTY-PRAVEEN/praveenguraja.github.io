Background

About

Dr. Praveen Kumar Guraja is a Tenure-Track Assistant Professor of Cybersecurity and Information Technology in the Department of Applied Computing & Geomatics at the Oregon Institute of Technology (Oregon Tech) with 16+ years of combined academic, research, and industry experience. He develops and teaches courses in cybersecurity, IT, and artificial intelligence, and created Oregon's first applied Bachelor of Science in Artificial Intelligence at a public university - NWCCU-approved March 2026, with its inaugural cohort launching Fall 2026. He redesigned the B.S. in Cybersecurity and B.S. in IT programs to align with ABET CAC standards, and serves as Lab Manager for the Applied Computing Lab and founded the Oregon Tech Cybersecurity Community Clinic (OTC³), Oregon's first member of the Consortium of Cybersecurity Clinics led by UC Berkeley and MIT.

Before joining Oregon Tech, Dr. Guraja served as Division Chair, Founding Program Coordinator, and Assistant Professor of Information Technology Management at the University of Pikeville UPIKE, where he launched undergraduate and graduate ITM programs and developed a second B.S. in AI along with an AI minor. His mentorship helped UPIKE's IT Club earn multiple national USITCC awards, including Student Organization of the Year (2025).

Dr. Guraja's research focuses on the intersection of AI, cybersecurity, and data analytics. He has published in peer-reviewed journals, authored book chapters for Taylor & Francis/CRC Press on generative AI and agentic AI systems, and presented at national and international conferences alongside speakers from Microsoft, Meta, and Google. His grant activity exceeds $3 million in funded and pending awards, including NSF and other federal and state funded projects.

He has received numerous honors, including the AAAI/ACM SIGAI Innovative AI Education Award (EAAI-26, Singapore), the Provost Award for Faculty Excellence and Leadership, the ACA Faculty Fellowship, and induction into Epsilon Pi Tau. He is a Google Gemini Certified Educator and serves on the Editorial Board of the Journal of Machine Learning and Applications.

A first-generation college graduate with over a decade of industry experience at ADP, Unisys, and Genpact, Dr. Guraja actively serves as Session Chair and Best Talk Award Judge at AAAI-26, IEEE SSIT Education Committee member, and reviewer for international journals, inspiring the next generation of innovators in cybersecurity, IT, and AI.
